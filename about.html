About Me

This is my first Pc build for a customer and I thought id share my expernce and thoughts about it.

PC Build Thought Process (about me) Building a personal computer (PC) takes time and problem-solving skills. This challenged me to think critically and solve issues that arose during the process. I knew it would be tricky at times, but I was ready to give it my best shot. My journey into PC building started with excitement, but I was also nervous because it was my first time building a PC for someone else, especially for someone who needed it to do their job. There was a lot at stake.

Over the summer, my dads friend asked him if I would like to help him with a project. My dad told me about the idea and I agreed to it. The goal was to help build a PC in under a week. I got the parts on wednesday from my dads friend. I started by unboxing everything and started in my basement. The first step of this project was to get out the motherboard and case. The case for where the parts would be stored, and the motherboard for everything to be connected and to work properly. I got the ram and CPU, and then put them in the motherboard socket. When putting the CPU in, you want to be careful because you could bend one of the tiny thousands of gold pins that are on the CPU. I inserted the CPU in and it clicked which was a good thing knowing it was in and not out of place. I grabbed the two ram sticks. Personal Computers are usually around $1.5k to about $3k depending on the parts but since this was for a programmer he needed a lot of ram and storage and processing. I took the RAM and put them into the slots A2 and B2. After a couple of hours working on the Personal Computers, and getting things into place and checking for things I took out the power supply and the cooler. This cooler was way different than the coolers I have worked on in the past. This was bigger and had more parts to it and more steps. I grabbed it and I took the three fans and decided on what I wanted the fans to do whether they were intake fans, which brings cool air into the pc to flow and cool down) or out take fans (taking the hot air out of the PC,. I decided on two in and one out. You usually want more cooling than outtake . When adding this cooler there were these brackets that came with them but there were multiple kinds of brackets for different types of motherboards. When I put the block aka the cooler part. It got on me thinking it was on and well fitted.

This was the test to see if it would work or not. I turned it on and it posted and then shut off instantly. I didn't know what happened. I checked for 20 mins and I tried something I never would've thought of. I put a card under the block and the CPU. The card slid straight through and I found out I had put on the wrong brackets. After about an hour or so of working on the pc. I put the new brackets on and succeeded. This Pc build showed me not to quit but rather keep going and not be satisfied until I had figured it out. I was glad to learn and gain more experience in building. This project was fun and I got paid for it as well. It was nice to help someone out but also gain some knowledge. And yes, I finished the PC in three days.
